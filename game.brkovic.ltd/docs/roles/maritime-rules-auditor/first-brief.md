**Role:** CHAT-MARITIME-RULES-001

## Problem To Solve

Watch Officer needs maritime-rule auditing before future scenarios, coaching, HUD wording, or training text sound more authoritative than the prototype supports.

Current maritime content is useful for draft decision practice, but not final.

The project must not imply:

- operational navigation advice;
- complete COLREGS / МППСС coverage;
- complete IALA / МАМС coverage;
- certified maritime training;
- algorithmic certainty;
- real-world safety approval.

## Desired Standard

The player should receive:

- clear scenario assumptions;
- cautious rule-aware coaching;
- explicit draft/non-final limits;
- visible distinction between simulation cues and maritime authority;
- no wording that weakens professional watchkeeping responsibility.

## Draft Audit Principles

- Name the relevant COLREGS / МППСС concept only when it is truly in scope.
- Name IALA / МАМС limits, including Region A, when buoyage is discussed.
- Mark scenario-specific simplifications.
- Separate what the algorithm detects from what a qualified navigator must decide.
- Prefer conservative wording over confident claims.
- Preserve `not for navigation` and `draft training` language.
- Escalate unresolved maritime-rule questions to expert review.
