## Why This Role Exists

Watch Officer has working prototype logic, HUD states, decision coaching, local Web export, and staged-public workflow.

The next risk is visual drift:

- too technical and thin like CAD;
- too cute like a cartoon;
- too harsh for long attention;
- too noisy for learning;
- too abstract to feel maritime.

This role protects perception quality before future visual/UI/art passes.

## Current Direction

Target:

```text
soft professional maritime simulator
```

Meaning:

- readable like a serious training tool;
- smooth like a modern game;
- calm enough for learning;
- maritime enough to feel credible;
- simplified enough for short sessions.

## Reference Lessons

Use these as conceptual anchors, not direct style copies:

- Mini Metro: clarity and system readability, but avoid thin transit-map engineering look.
- Dorfromantik: calm, low stress, soft perception, but avoid fairy-tale/cozy style.
- ISLANDERS: clean minimal strategy and low pressure, but avoid toy-like city-builder tone.
- Sailaway: adult maritime credibility, but avoid full sim complexity and visual overload.

## Known Watch Officer Constraints

- Existing Godot build is greybox/prototype.
- Current visual pass is not final.
- Training claims remain draft/non-final.
- The player must see safe water, shallow risk, target vector, ownship state, warnings, and coaching cues without visual overload.
